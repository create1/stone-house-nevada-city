About Stone House

Short Version (50 words)

Stone House is a private event venue in Nevada City, California, housed in an 1857 Gold Rush–era building. Featuring six distinct spaces, a seed-oil-free farm-to-table kitchen, and original stone architecture, Stone House hosts weddings and celebrations for 2–300+ guests across multiple indoor and outdoor environments.
Suitable for event listings, directories, and brief mentions.

Long Version (120 words)

Stone House is a private wedding and event venue located in the heart of Nevada City, California's historic downtown. Built in 1857 during the Gold Rush, the building's original stone walls, timber beams, and hand-laid masonry create an atmosphere that no amount of modern construction can replicate. The venue offers six distinct spaces — The Great Hall, The Showroom (with built-in stage), The Penthouse, The Parlor, The Cavern, and The Courtyard — accommodating everything from two-person elopements to 300-guest celebrations. Stone House's in-house kitchen operates on a strict seed-oil-free philosophy, cooking exclusively with butter, olive oil, tallow, and coconut oil. All menus feature locally sourced, seasonal ingredients from Nevada County farms and Northern California purveyors.
Suitable for feature articles, venue profiles, and press releases.
Venue Details

Key Information

Location: 107 Sacramento Street, Nevada City, CA 95959

Region: Gold Country / Sierra Foothills, Northern California. 60 miles northeast of Sacramento, 90 miles from Lake Tahoe.

Event Types: Weddings, rehearsal dinners, elopements, corporate events, private celebrations, live performances.

Capacity: 2 guests (elopement) to 300+ guests (full venue buyout). The Great Hall seats 60–108. The Showroom holds 120–150 ceremony-style. The Penthouse seats 55–85.

Cuisine: In-house farm-to-table kitchen. Three menu tiers: Prospector ($69–$79/pp), Brewmaster ($79–$89/pp), Mother Lode ($89–$99/pp). 100% seed-oil-free. Full bar program with local wines and craft cocktails.

History: Constructed in 1857 during the California Gold Rush. One of the oldest standing commercial structures in Nevada City. Original stone walls preserved throughout.
