The Great Hall

The Heart of the Celebration

The largest interior space at Stone House. An irregularly shaped room — a distinctive wedge that narrows from 35 feet at the back wall to 19 feet at the entrance — with original 1857 stone walls on every side. The shape creates visual drama and natural intimacy even at full capacity.

Reception 90 – 108

Full Banquet

9 rectangular tables in a 3x3 grid with cocktail high-tops. Maximum seated capacity for plated or family-style service.

Reception 60 – 72

Medium Banquet

6 rectangular tables in a 2x3 grid. More spacious feel, room for a dance floor or lounge area within the hall.

Buffet 80 – 96

Buffet Service

8 tables with a dedicated buffet station along the service wall. Natural flow from food to seating.

Cocktail 120 – 150

Standing Reception

Tables cleared, cocktail high-tops and lounge furniture. Maximum standing capacity for cocktail-style events.

The Showroom

Where Ceremonies Come Alive

The Showroom features a built-in stage with curtain — originally a performance space, now the most popular ceremony location at Stone House. The angled left wall follows the building's original stone footprint, creating an intimate amphitheater effect where every guest has a clear sightline.

Ceremony 120 – 150

Wedding Ceremony

Chairs in two sections with center aisle. Altar positioned at the stage. Curved seating follows the room's natural shape for unobstructed views.

Theater 80 – 100

Theater Seating

Rows of chairs facing the stage. Ideal for presentations, live performances, or speeches during the reception.

The Penthouse

The Upstairs Experience

A clean rectangular room on the upper level with two full bar stations at the entrance. The Penthouse works as a self-contained reception space for mid-size events, or as a second dining room for larger celebrations that flow between floors.

Reception 70 – 80

Full Banquet + Head Table

Long head table for the wedding party, 6 rectangular tables for guests. Two bar stations at entry. Maximum seated capacity.

Buffet 75 – 85

Buffet Banquet

Same head table and dining tables, buffet station added along the upper wall. Slightly tighter table spacing with natural buffet flow.

Intimate 55 – 65

Intimate Dinner

Smaller head table (6 seats), 6 guest tables with generous spacing. Room to breathe — perfect for rehearsal dinners and smaller celebrations.

Cocktail 80 – 100

Standing Reception

Tables cleared for cocktail high-tops and lounge seating. Both bars operational. Ideal cocktail hour space while the Great Hall is flipped for dinner.

The Parlor

The Downstairs Bar

The Parlor is Stone House's dedicated cocktail and lounge space on the ground level. An L-shaped bar anchors the right side of the room, with round tables, lounge seating, and a water station creating a relaxed atmosphere that's the natural complement to the more formal dining spaces above.

Cocktail 40 – 60

Cocktail Hour

Round tables (4-tops), lounge clusters with sofas and side tables, L-shaped bar. The go-to space for welcome drinks and cocktail hour while other spaces are being set.

Lounge 30 – 40

After-Party Lounge

Reconfigured for late-night vibes — lounge seating forward, bar in full swing. When the reception wraps, the party moves here.

Planning Your Layout

How Couples Use These Spaces

Most Stone House weddings use three or more spaces throughout the evening. Here are the most popular flows:

Classic Flow (100–150 guests)

Ceremony in The Showroom → Cocktail hour in The Parlor → Dinner and dancing in The Great Hall

Summer Celebration (150–200 guests)

Ceremony in The Courtyard → Cocktails in The Parlor → Reception split between The Great Hall + The Penthouse

Intimate Dinner (40–70 guests)

Ceremony in The Showroom → Everything else in The Penthouse (dinner, toasts, dancing, bars all in one room)

Full Venue Buyout (200–300 guests)

All six spaces in play — ceremony, multiple dining zones, cocktail bars on both levels, outdoor overflow, late-night lounge

Your coordinator handles the details. Tell us your guest count and your vision — we'll propose a layout that uses the right combination of spaces. Every configuration has been tested in real events.
