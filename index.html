Handbuilt in 1857.
Reimagined for today.

Today, 10,000 square feet of beautifully restored space hosts weddings, celebrations, and gatherings where elevated cuisine and irreplaceable architecture converge.

Frequently Asked

Everything you need
to know

How far in advance should I book Stone House?
We recommend booking 12–18 months in advance for Saturday dates, especially for spring and fall. Summer Saturdays are typically the first to go. That said, we do have some last-minute availability — check your date and we'll let you know within 24 hours.
What does "seed oil free" mean for the catering?
We cook exclusively with high-quality fats — butter, olive oil, avocado oil, tallow, and coconut oil. No vegetable oil, canola oil, soybean oil, or other industrial seed oils. This isn't a trend for us — it's a genuine commitment to serving food that tastes better and is better for your guests.
Can we bring our own vendors?
Absolutely. You're welcome to work with any licensed and insured vendors. We also have a preferred vendor list of photographers, florists, DJs, and planners who know the venue well and consistently deliver exceptional work. We're happy to make introductions.
Is there parking on-site?
Yes. We have dedicated parking adjacent to the venue, with additional public parking within one block. For larger events, we recommend coordinating a shuttle from nearby hotels — we can connect you with trusted transportation partners.
Can we do the ceremony and reception at Stone House?
Yes — and this is one of our most popular arrangements. With six unique spaces, you can have your ceremony in The Courtyard, cocktail hour in The Parlor, and reception in The Great Hall — all without your guests ever leaving the building. No shuttles, no logistics, no stress.
What's the deposit and payment structure?
We require a 25% deposit to hold your date, with the balance due 60 days before your event. We accept all major credit cards and ACH transfers. Download our complete pricing guide for full details on payment schedules and cancellation policies.
